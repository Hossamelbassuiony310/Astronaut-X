Summary

Astronaut is a highly trained professional who travels into space to conduct research, perform experiments, and operate spacecraft. Derived from the Greek words "astron" (meaning star) and "nautes" (meaning sailor), astronauts are truly “sailors of the stars.” They work in space environments aboard spacecraft, such as the International Space Station (ISS), and are responsible for maintaining equipment, conducting spacewalks, and gathering data crucial for scientific discovery.

Features

Blackness

Without an atmosphere to scatter sunlight, space appears black, even in the presence of a nearby star like the Sun.

No Friction

This is a fundamental concept of Newton’s First Law of Motion—objects in space will keep moving indefinitely without resistance.

Cosmic Radiation

Earth’s atmosphere and magnetic field protect us from much of this radiation, but in space, astronauts are exposed to these potentially harmful particles.

No Atmosphere

Without an atmosphere, there is no breathable air or pressure to support life, making it a hostile environment for humans.

Blackness

Without an atmosphere to scatter sunlight, space appears black, even in the presence of a nearby star like the Sun.

Vacuum

The vacuum of space also affects heat transfer, as there are no molecules to conduct heat, making it difficult for heat to dissipate.
